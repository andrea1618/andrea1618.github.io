Research

Working Papers

Policy Effects on Human Capital Development: Evidence from Early Childhood Education Programs

Working Paper 2024
Zheyu Yang, Jane Smith, Robert Johnson

This paper examines the long-term effects of early childhood education policies on human capital accumulation and labor market outcomes. Using a regression discontinuity design with administrative data from a major policy reform, we find that access to high-quality early childhood education programs increases educational attainment by 0.8 years and lifetime earnings by 12%. The effects are particularly pronounced for children from disadvantaged backgrounds, suggesting that such policies can be effective tools for reducing inequality.

Labor Market Dynamics in Developing Economies: A Structural Approach

Under Review 2024
Zheyu Yang, Maria Garcia

We develop and estimate a structural model of labor market dynamics in developing economies, focusing on the role of informal employment and limited labor mobility. Our model incorporates search frictions, wage bargaining, and heterogeneous worker productivity. Using panel data from three Latin American countries, we find that reducing search frictions could increase formal employment by 15% and aggregate productivity by 8%.

The Impact of Minimum Wage Policies on Employment and Firm Dynamics

Draft 2024
Zheyu Yang

This study analyzes the heterogeneous effects of minimum wage increases on employment and firm dynamics across different industries and firm sizes. Using comprehensive administrative data and variation in minimum wage policies across states, I document that small firms in labor-intensive industries experience the largest negative employment effects, while larger firms adjust primarily through reduced hiring rather than layoffs.

Publications

Education Returns and Labor Market Segmentation: Evidence from Urban China

Published 2023
Journal of Development Economics, Vol. 162, 2023
Zheyu Yang, Li Wei, David Thompson

We examine the returns to education in China's segmented labor market, distinguishing between state-owned enterprises, private firms, and the informal sector. Our analysis reveals significant heterogeneity in education returns across sectors, with the highest returns observed in the private sector. These findings have important implications for understanding inequality and economic development in transitional economies.

Work in Progress

Digital Technology Adoption and Productivity Growth in Small Firms

In Progress 2025
Zheyu Yang, Sarah Chen

This project examines how digital technology adoption affects productivity growth in small and medium-sized enterprises. We are constructing a novel dataset combining firm-level productivity data with detailed information on technology adoption patterns.

Gender Wage Gaps and Occupational Sorting: A Cross-Country Analysis

In Progress 2025
Zheyu Yang, Anna Rodriguez, Michael Brown

We investigate the relationship between gender wage gaps and occupational segregation across developed countries, exploring how institutional factors and cultural norms influence these patterns.
