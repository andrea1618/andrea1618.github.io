Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy centers on making economics accessible and relevant to students from diverse backgrounds. I believe that effective economics education should combine rigorous theoretical foundations with real-world applications that help students understand how economic principles shape policy decisions and everyday life.

I strive to create an inclusive classroom environment where students feel comfortable asking questions and engaging in critical thinking. I use a variety of teaching methods, including interactive lectures, group discussions, empirical exercises, and case studies, to accommodate different learning styles and keep students engaged.

Particularly in my econometrics and research methods courses, I emphasize hands-on learning with statistical software and real data. I believe that students learn best when they can immediately apply theoretical concepts to practical problems and see the relevance of what they're learning to current policy debates and research questions.
